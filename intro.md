# California Solar Adoption & Grid Displacement Analysis

## Research Question

Does geographic clustering of behind-the-meter (BTM) residential solar adoption in California (2015–2023) predict measurable changes in grid stress — specifically faster net-load ramps, higher curtailment rates, and more frequent negative wholesale prices?

## Motivation

California leads the U.S. in distributed solar deployment, yet the grid-level consequences of spatially concentrated rooftop PV remain empirically understudied. System operators observe the duck curve deepening and curtailment events multiplying, but attribution to local adoption density — rather than to aggregate installed capacity — requires panel data methods that account for ZIP-level fixed effects and spatial autocorrelation.

## Scope

- **Geography**: California IOU territories (PG&E, SCE, SDG&E). POUs (LADWP, SMUD) excluded.
- **Time**: 2015–2023 (covers NEM 1.0 → NEM 2.0 → NBT policy transitions).
- **Unit of analysis**: ZIP code × month panel (~4,000 ZCTAs × 108 months).
- **Grid metrics**: System-level CAISO data (demand, solar/wind generation, curtailment, LMPs).
